Dosa

Description:

The dosa is a delicious, thin, savory crepe originating from South India, made from a fermented batter of ground rice and black gram lentils. It is celebrated for its versatile textures, ranging from wafer-thin and crispy golden to soft and spongy.

Ingredients:

  • Rice: 2 cups (Idli rice or parboiled rice works well)
  • Urad Dal (skinned black gram): ½ cup
  • Fenugreek Seeds (Methi): ¼ teaspoon
  • Poha (Flattened Rice): ¼ cup (for softness)
  • Salt: To taste (add after fermentation)
  • Water: For soaking and grinding
  • Oil or Ghee: For cooking

Steps:

  1. Heat Tawa: Heat a non-stick or cast-iron griddle (tawa) over medium heat. Sprinkle a few drops of water; they should sizzle and evaporate.
  2. Spread Batter: Stir fermented batter, add a little water if too thick. Pour a ladleful onto the hot tawa and spread in a circular motion to form a thin crepe.
  3. Cook: Drizzle oil/ghee around the edges and center. Cook until golden brown and crispy, with small holes appearing.
  4. Serve: Fold and serve hot with coconut chutney or sambar.
